# Throat Disorders

<p>Your throat is a tube that carries food to your esophagus and air to your windpipe and larynx. The technical name for your throat is the pharynx. </p>

<p>Throat problems are common. You've probably had a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sorethroat.html">sore throat</a>. The cause is usually a viral infection, but other causes include allergies, infection with <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/streptococcalinfections.html">strep</a>  bacteria or the leaking of stomach acids back up into the esophagus, called <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/gerd.html">GERD</a>. </p>

<p>Other problems that affect the throat include:</p><ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/tonsillitis.html">Tonsillitis</a> - inflammation of the tonsils</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/throatcancer.html">Cancer</a> </li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/croup.html">Croup</a> - inflammation, usually in small children, which causes a barking cough</li>
<li>Laryngitis - swelling of the voice box, which can cause a hoarse voice or loss of voice</li>
</ul>

<p>Most throat problems are minor and go away on their own. Treatments, when needed, depend on the problem.  </p>
