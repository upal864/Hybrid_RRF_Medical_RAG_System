# Health Problems in Pregnancy

<h3>What are health problems in pregnancy?</h3>
<p>A health problem in <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pregnancy.html">pregnancy</a> is any disease or condition that could affect your health or the <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fetalhealthanddevelopment.html">health of your fetus</a>. Some health problems may make it more likely that you will have a high-risk pregnancy. A high-risk pregnancy is one in which you, your fetus, or both are at higher risk for health problems than in a typical pregnancy.</p> 
<p>But just because you have health problems, it doesn't mean that you, or your fetus, will have a problem during the pregnancy. Taking care of yourself and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/preconceptioncare.html">getting early</a> and regular <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/prenatalcare.html">prenatal care</a> from a health care provider may help you reduce pregnancy risks from health problems.</p>

<h3>What raises my risk for health problems during pregnancy?</h3>
<p>Every pregnancy has some risk of problems, but your lifestyle, as well as factors like certain conditions and health issues, can raise that risk, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>A health condition you had before you got pregnant</li> 
<li>A health condition you develop during pregnancy</li> 
<li>A pregnancy with <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/twinstripletsmultiplebirths.html">more than one baby</a></li> 
<li>A health problem that happened during a previous pregnancy and could happen again</li>  
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pregnancyandsubstanceuse.html">Substance use during pregnancy</a></li> 
<li>Being over age 35</li> 
</ul>

<p>Any of these can affect your health, the health of your fetus, or both.</p>

<h3>Can chronic health conditions cause problems in pregnancy?</h3>
<p>Every pregnancy is different. If you have specific risks in one pregnancy, it doesn't mean that you will have them in another. But, if you have a chronic condition, you should talk to your provider about how to minimize your risk before you get pregnant. Once you are pregnant, you may need a health care team to monitor your pregnancy. Some common conditions that can complicate a pregnancy include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/highbloodpressureinpregnancy.html">High blood pressure</a></li>
<li><a href="polycysticovarysyndrome.html" tid="5912">Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/kidneydiseases.html">Kidney problems</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/autoimmunediseases.html">Autoimmune disorders</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/thyroiddiseases.html">Thyroid disease</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/obesity.html">Obesity</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hivandpregnancy.html">HIV</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/tumorsandpregnancy.html">Cancer</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/infectionsandpregnancy.html">Infections</a></li>
</ul>

<p>Other conditions that may make pregnancy risky can develop during pregnancy - for example, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabetesandpregnancy.html">gestational diabetes</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/rhincompatibility.html">Rh incompatibility</a>.</p> 

<h3>Can health problems in pregnancy be prevented?</h3>
<p>You may be able to lower your risk of certain health problems by making healthy lifestyle changes before you get pregnant. These can include reaching a healthy weight, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/quittingsmoking.html">not smoking</a>, and managing any health conditions.</p>  
<p>However, health problems during pregnancy are not always preventable. Some chronic conditions can be treated and controlled, while others aren't treatable and carry a higher risk than usual, even if the health problem is well-managed.</p>  
<p>Share your symptoms with your provider for early detection and treatment of health problems. Sometimes, it's hard to know what's normal. Some symptoms, like <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/nauseaandvomiting.html">nausea</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/backpain.html">back pain</a>, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fatigue.html">fatigue</a>, are common during pregnancy, while other symptoms, like <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vaginalbleeding.html">vaginal bleeding</a> or a severe or long-lasting <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/headache.html">headache</a>, can be signs of a problem. Call your provider to let them know if something is bothering or worrying you.</p>
