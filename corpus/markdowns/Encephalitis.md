# Encephalitis

<h3>What is encephalitis? </h3>
<p>Encephalitis is inflammation (swelling) of the brain. It can happen when an infection or medical condition that affects the brain activates your immune system. The inflammation can cause a wide range of symptoms. In extreme cases, it can lead to brain damage, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/stroke.html">stroke</a>, or even death.</p>

<h3>What causes encephalitis?</h3>
<p>There are different types of encephalitis, based on what the cause is. The two main types are infectious encephalitis and autoimmune encephalitis.</p>

<p><strong>Infectious encephalitis</strong> is usually caused by a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/viralinfections.html">virus</a>. In fact, viruses are the most common cause of encephalitis. Some of the different viruses that cause it include:</p><ul>
<li>Herpes viruses, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/herpessimplex.html">herpes simplex</a> (HSV), the Epstein-Barr virus (which causes <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/infectiousmononucleosis.html">infectious mononucleosis</a>) and the varicella-zoster virus (which causes <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/chickenpox.html">chickenpox</a>).</li>
<li>Viruses you can get if you are bitten by an infected <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/tickbites.html">tick</a>, such as tick-borne encephalitis (TBE virus) and Powassan virus.</li>
<li>Viruses you can get if you are bitten by an infected <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/mosquitobites.html">mosquito</a>, such as eastern equine encephalitis virus, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/westnilevirus.html">West Nile virus</a>, and La Crosse virus.</li>
<li>Enteroviruses, which are a common group of viruses that mostly cause mild illness or respiratory infection. These infections usually happen in the summer and fall.</li>
</ul>

<p><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bacterialinfections.html">Bacteria</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fungalinfections.html">fungi</a>, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/parasiticdiseases.html">parasites</a> can also cause infectious encephalitis. But this is not common.</p>

<p><strong>Autoimmune encephalitis</strong> happens when your immune system mistakenly attacks healthy brain cells. It can be triggered by conditions such as certain cancers, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/benigntumors.html">benign tumors</a>, and infections. Sometimes the cause is not known.</p>

<h3>Who is more likely to get encephalitis?</h3>
<p>Anyone can get encephalitis, but you are more likely to get it if you:</p>
<ul>
<li>Have a weakened immune system, for example from having <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hiv.html">HIV</a> or taking certain medicines. These could include medicines taken after an <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/organtransplantation.html">organ transplant</a>, certain <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cancerchemotherapy.html">chemotherapy</a> medicines, and specialized treatments for certain <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/autoimmunediseases.html">autoimmune diseases</a>.</li>
<li>Are a young child or older adult.</li>
<li>Live in areas where there are ticks and mosquitoes that carry viruses that can cause encephalitis.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the symptoms of encephalitis?</h3>
<p>The symptoms of encephalitis can vary a lot, depending on how severe it is. Many people do not have any symptoms. Others may have mild flu-like symptoms such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fever.html">fever</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fatigue.html">fatigue</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/headache.html">headache</a>, or body aches. If encephalitis becomes more serious, it can cause:</p>
<ul>
<li>Severe headache</li>
<li>Stiff neck</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/nauseaandvomiting.html">Vomiting</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/seizures.html">Seizures</a></li>
<li>Behavior changes</li>
<li>Drowsiness</li>
<li>Muscle weakness</li>
<li>Partial <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/paralysis.html">paralysis</a> in your arms and legs</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/coma.html">Coma</a></li>
</ul>

<p>Encephalitis can be dangerous in infants. Their symptoms may include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fever</li>
<li>Lethargy (weakness or drowsiness)</li>
<li>Poor feeding</li>
<li>Vomiting</li>
<li>Body stiffness</li>
<li>Unusual irritability or crying</li>
<li>A full or bulging fontanel (the soft spot on the top of the head)</li>
</ul>

<p>If you or your child is having symptoms of encephalitis, it's important to get medical care right away.</p>

<h3>How is encephalitis diagnosed?</h3>
<p>To find out if you have encephalitis, your health care provider:</p>
<ul>
<li>Will do a physical exam</li> 
<li>Will take your medical history, which includes asking about your symptoms</li>
<li>May do a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/lab-tests/neurological-exam/">neurologic exam</a></li> 
<li>May order <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diagnosticimaging.html">imaging tests</a>, such as a brain <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/ctscans.html">CT scan</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/mriscans.html">MRI</a></li>
<li>May order an EEG (electroencephalography), which use small electric sensors to measure your brain activity</li>
<li>May order blood and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/lab-tests/cerebrospinal-fluid-csf-analysis/">cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) tests</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the treatments for encephalitis?</h3>
<p>Most people with encephalitis will need treatment in the hospital. Depending on the cause, treatments may include antiviral medicines, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/antibiotics.html">antibiotics</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/steroids.html">corticosteroids</a>, and other medicines.</p>

<p>For some types of encephalitis, there is no medicine to treat it. But rest, nutrition, and fluids can help your body fight the infection and relieve symptoms. </p>

<p>Some people may need physical, speech, and occupational therapy once the illness is under control.</p>

<h3>Can encephalitis be prevented?</h3>
<p>There are steps you can take to help prevent encephalitis that is caused by infections:</p>
<ul>
<li>Use <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/germsandhygiene.html">good hygiene</a>, including washing your hands often with soap and water.</li>
<li>Don't share food, drinks, utensils, and glasses with other people.</li>
<li>Get <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vaccines.html">vaccines</a> for viruses that can cause encephalitis.</li>
<li>Avoid mosquito and tick bites, for example by:
  <ul>
  <li>Wearing insect repellent with DEET or another U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)-registered insect repellent. Make sure to follow the instructions for using the repellant.</li>
  <li>Wearing clothes that cover your arms, legs and feet.</li>
  <li>Treating your clothing and gear with products containing 0.5% permethrin before you go in grassy or woody areas.</li>
  </ul>
</li>
</ul>
