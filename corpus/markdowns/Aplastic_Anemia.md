# Aplastic Anemia

<h3>What is aplastic anemia?</h3>
<p>Aplastic <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/anemia.html"> anemia</a> is a rare but serious blood disorder.  If you have it, your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bonemarrowdiseases.html">bone marrow</a> doesn't make enough new blood cells. It happens when there is damage to stem cells inside your bone marrow. There are different types of aplastic anemia, including Fanconi anemia.</p>  

<h3>What causes aplastic anemia?</h3>
<p>The causes of aplastic anemia can include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/autoimmunediseases.html">Autoimmune disorders</a>, which are the most common cause</li>
<li>Certain inherited gene changes, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/genetics/condition/fanconi-anemia/">the one that can cause Fanconi anemia</a></li>
<li>Toxic substances, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pesticides.html">pesticides</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/arsenic.html">arsenic</a>, and benzene</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/radiationtherapy.html">Radiation therapy</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cancerchemotherapy.html">chemotherapy</a> for cancer</li>
<li>Certain medicines</li>
<li>Viral infections such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hepatitis.html">hepatitis</a>, Epstein-Barr virus, or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hiv.html">HIV</a></li>
<li>Pregnancy</li>
</ul>
<p>In many people, the cause is unknown. This is called idiopathic aplastic anemia.</p> 

<h3>What are the symptoms of aplastic anemia?</h3>
<p>Aplastic anemia can develop suddenly or slowly. It can be mild or severe. The symptoms of aplastic anemia can include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fatigue.html">Fatigue</a></li>
<li>Weakness</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/dizzinessandvertigo.html">Dizziness</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/breathingproblems.html">Shortness of breath</a></li>
<li>Easy <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bruises.html">bruising</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bleeding.html">bleeding</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What other problems can aplastic anemia cause?</h3>
<p>Aplastic anemia can cause other problems, including frequent infections and bleeding. It raises your risk of developing a serious <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/blooddisorders.html">blood disorder</a>.</p>
<p>If not treated, aplastic anemia can also lead to heart problems such as an <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/arrhythmia.html">arrhythmia</a> (a problem with the rate or rhythm of your heartbeat), an enlarged heart, or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartfailure.html">heart failure</a>.</p> 

<h3>How is aplastic anemia diagnosed?</h3>
<p>To find out if you have aplastic anemia, your doctor will:</p><p>
</p><ul>
<li>Take your medical and your family medical histories</li>
<li>Do a physical exam</li>
<li>Order tests, such as tests to check if you have low numbers of cells in your bone marrow and blood</li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the treatments for aplastic anemia?</h3> 
<p>If you have aplastic anemia, your doctor will create a treatment plan for you. The plan will be based on how severe the anemia is and what is causing it. Treatments can include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Blood <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bloodtransfusionanddonation.html">transfusions</a></li> 
<li>Blood and marrow <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/stemcells.html">stem cell transplants</a></li>
<li>Medicines to suppress your immune system</li>
</ul>

<p>Because of the risk of blood disorders, your doctor will monitor your condition and screen you for blood disorders regularly.</p>
