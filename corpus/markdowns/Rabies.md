# Rabies

<p>Rabies is a deadly animal disease caused by a virus. It can happen in wild animals, including raccoons, skunks, bats and foxes, or in dogs, cats or farm animals. People get it from the <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/animalbites.html">bite</a> of an infected animal. </p>

<p>In people, symptoms of rabies include fever, headache and fatigue, then confusion, hallucinations and paralysis. Once the symptoms begin, the disease is usually fatal. A series of shots can prevent rabies in people exposed to the virus. You need to get them right away. If an animal bites you, wash the wound well; then get medical care. </p>

<p>To help prevent rabies:</p><ul>
<li>	Vaccinate your pet. Rabies vaccines are available for dogs, cats and farm animals</li>
<li>	Don't let pets roam</li>
<li>	Don't approach stray animals. Animals with rabies might be aggressive and vicious, or tired and weak</li>
</ul>
