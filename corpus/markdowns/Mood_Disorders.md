# Mood Disorders

<p>Most people feel sad or irritable from time to time. They may say they're in a bad mood. A mood disorder is different. It affects a person's everyday emotional state. Nearly one in ten people aged 18 and older have mood disorders. These include <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/depression.html">depression</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bipolardisorder.html">bipolar disorder</a> (also called manic depression).</p>

<p>Mood disorders can increase a person's risk for heart disease, diabetes, and other diseases. Treatments include medication, psychotherapy, or a combination of both. With treatment, most people with mood disorders can lead productive lives.</p>
