# Usher Syndrome

<p>Usher syndrome is an inherited disease that causes serious <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hearingdisordersanddeafness.html">hearing loss</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/retinaldisorders.html">retinitis pigmentosa</a>, an eye disorder that causes your vision to get worse over time. It is the most common condition that affects both hearing and vision.</p>

<p>There are three types of Usher syndrome:</p>
<ul>
<li>People with type I are deaf from birth and have severe balance problems from a young age. Vision problems usually start by age 10 and lead to blindness.</li>
<li>People with type II have moderate to severe hearing loss and normal balance. Vision problems start in the early teens and get worse more slowly than in type I.</li>
<li>People with type III are born with normal hearing and near-normal balance but develop vision problems and then hearing loss.</li>
</ul>

<p>There is no cure. Tools such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hearingaids.html">hearing aids</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cochlearimplants.html">cochlear implants</a> can help some people. Training such as Braille instruction, low-vision services, or auditory training can also help.</p>
