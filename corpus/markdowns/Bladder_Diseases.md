# Bladder Diseases

<p>The bladder is a hollow organ in your lower abdomen that stores urine. Many conditions can affect your bladder. Some common ones are:</p><ul>
<li>Cystitis - inflammation of the bladder, often from an <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/urinarytractinfections.html">infection</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/urinaryincontinence.html">Urinary incontinence</a> - loss of bladder control</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/overactivebladder.html">Overactive bladder</a> - a condition in which the bladder squeezes urine out at the wrong time</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/interstitialcystitis.html">Interstitial cystitis</a> - a chronic problem that causes bladder pain and frequent, urgent urination</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bladdercancer.html">Bladder cancer</a></li>
</ul>

<p>Doctors diagnose bladder diseases using different tests. These include <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/urinalysis.html">urine tests</a>, x-rays, and an examination of the bladder wall with a scope called a cystoscope. Treatment depends on the cause of the problem. It may include medicines and, in severe cases, surgery. </p>
