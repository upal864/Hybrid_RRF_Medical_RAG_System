# Preconception Care

<p>If you are trying to have a baby or are just thinking about it, it is not too early to prepare for a safe pregnancy and a healthy baby. You should see your health care provider for preconception care.</p>

<p>Preconception care is care you receive before you get pregnant. Your health care provider can help you figure out which steps you need to take now, such as:</p><ul>
<li>Finding and taking care of any problems that might affect you and your baby later, like <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabetesandpregnancy.html">diabetes</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/highbloodpressureinpregnancy.html">high blood pressure</a></li>
<li>Increasing the amount of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/folicacid.html">folic acid</a> you get to prevent <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/neuraltubedefects.html">neural tube defects</a></li>
<li>Getting up to date on vaccines</li>
<li>Avoiding smoking, drinking alcohol, or taking drugs</li>
<li>Trying to reach a healthy weight</li>
</ul>

<p>By taking action on health issues before pregnancy, you can prevent many future problems for yourself and your baby. Once you're pregnant,  you'll get <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/prenatalcare.html">prenatal care</a> until your baby is born.</p>
