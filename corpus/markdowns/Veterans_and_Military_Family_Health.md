# Veterans and Military Family Health

<p>Service members and veterans may face some different <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/veteransandmilitaryhealth.html">health issues</a> than civilians. Their families may also face some unique challenges, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>Being separated from their loved ones.</li>
<li>Worrying about a loved ones' safety in combat zones.</li>
<li>Coping as a single parent while their partner is deployed.</li>
<li>Having to move frequently.</li> 
<li>Helping their loved one deal with Illnesses and injuries from combat, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/disabilities.html">disabilities</a>. This can lead to <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/caregiverhealth.html">caregiver stress</a>.</li>
<li>Helping their loved one cope with the mental health effects of military service. These could include <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/depression.html">depression</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/posttraumaticstressdisorder.html">post-traumatic stress disorder</a> (PTSD).</li>
</ul>

<p>Dealing with these challenges can be difficult. Some possible sources of help include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Getting support from your family and community</li>
<li>Using military outreach programs, which help families feel less isolated</li>
<li>Professional counseling</li> 
</ul>
