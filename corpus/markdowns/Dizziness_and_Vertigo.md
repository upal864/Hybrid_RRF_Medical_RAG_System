# Dizziness and Vertigo

<p>When you're dizzy, you may feel lightheaded, woozy, or disoriented. If you feel like you or the room are spinning, you have vertigo. These feelings may make you lose your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/balanceproblems.html">balance</a>.</p>

<p>Dizziness can have many different causes. A sudden drop in <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/lowbloodpressure.html">blood pressure</a> or being <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/dehydration.html">dehydrated</a> can make you dizzy. Many people feel lightheaded if they get up too quickly from sitting or lying down. Certain medicines and problems with your inner <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/eardisorders.html">ear</a> may cause dizziness. So can <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/motionsickness.html">motion sickness</a>. Sometimes dizziness can be a symptom of other disorders.</p>

<p>As people get older, they may have more health problems and take more medicines. This makes them more likely to have problems with dizziness and balance.</p>

<p>Dizziness usually gets better by itself or is easily treated. If you are dizzy often, you should see your health care provider to find the cause.</p>
