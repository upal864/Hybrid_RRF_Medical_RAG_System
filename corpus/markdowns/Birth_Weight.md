# Birth Weight

<h3>What is birth weight?</h3>
<p>Birth weight is the first weight of your baby, taken just after being born.  A low birth weight means that the baby is less than 5 pounds, 8 ounces. A high birth weight means that the baby is more than 8 pounds, 13 ounces.</p>  

<h3>What can cause low birth weight?</h3>
<p>A baby with a low birth weight can be born too small, too early (<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/prematurebabies.html">premature</a>), or both. This can happen for many different reasons. They include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/healthproblemsinpregnancy.html">Health problems in the pregnant parent</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pregnancyandmedicines.html">Taking certain medicines during pregnancy</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pregnancyandsubstanceuse.html">Substance use during pregnancy</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/infectionsandpregnancy.html">Infections during pregnancy</a></li>
<li>Being pregnant with <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/twinstripletsmultiplebirths.html">multiple babies</a></li>
<li>Problems with the placenta, the organ that brings oxygen and nutrients to the baby</li>
<li>Having small parents</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/geneticdisorders.html">Genetic conditions</a> in the baby</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/birthdefects.html">Birth defects</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What problems can low birth weight cause?</h3>
<p>Babies with low birth weight may be more at risk of certain health problems. They include immediate problems, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/breathingproblems.html">Breathing problems</a></li>
<li>Infections</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hypoglycemia.html">Hypoglycemia</a> (low blood sugar)</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/jaundice.html">Jaundice</a></li>
<li>Trouble keeping warm</li>
</ul>

<p>There is also a higher risk of longer-term problems, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Delayed motor and social development</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/learningdisabilities.html">Learning disabilities</a></li>
<li>Certain health conditions as an adult, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartdiseases.html">heart disease</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/highbloodpressure.html">high blood pressure</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabetes.html">diabetes</a>, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/obesity.html">obesity</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What can cause high birth weight?</h3>
<p>Causes of high birth weight can include:</p>
<ul> 
<li>Having big parents</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabetesandpregnancy.html">Diabetes in the pregnant parent</a></li>
<li>Certain genetic conditions</li>
<li>Too much weight gain during pregnancy</li>
</ul>

<h3>What problems can high birth weight cause?</h3>
<p>High birth weight can be a concern because it can make delivery of the baby difficult and raise the risk of birth injuries. The baby is at higher risk of problems with:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bloodglucose.html">Blood sugar</a></li>
<li>Breathing problems</li>
<li>Jaundice</li>
</ul>

<h3>Can birth weight problems be prevented?</h3>
<p>You may be able to prevent some birth weight problems by:</p>
<ul>
<li>Getting regular <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/prenatalcare.html">checkups during pregnancy</a></li>
<li>Getting the right amount of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pregnancyandnutrition.html">nutrition</a> and calories during pregnancy</li>
<li>Controlling your blood sugar if you have diabetes</li>
<li>Avoiding substance use during pregnancy</li>
</ul>
