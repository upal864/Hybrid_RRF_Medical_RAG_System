# Rural Health Concerns

<p>Around 15% of people in the United States live in rural areas. There are many different reasons why you might choose to live in a rural community. You may want a lower cost of living and a slower pace of life. You may enjoy having access to big, open spaces for recreation. Rural areas are less crowded and can offer more privacy. You may choose a rural area so that you can live near your family and friends.</p> 

<p>But there are also challenges to living in a rural area, including when it comes to taking care of your health.  Compared to urban areas, rural communities tend to have:</p><ul>
<li>Higher poverty rates.</li>
<li>A higher percentage of older adults, who are more likely to have chronic health problems.</li>
<li>More residents without health insurance.</li>
<li>Less access to health care. For example, clinics and hospitals may be far away.</li>
<li>Higher rates of certain substance use, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/smoking.html">cigarette smoking</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/opioidsandopioidusedisorderoud.html">opioid</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/methamphetamine.html">methamphetamine</a> misuse.</li>
<li>Higher rates of chronic health problems such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/highbloodpressure.html">high blood pressure</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/obesity.html">obesity</a>.</li>
<li>More exposure to environmental hazards, such as chemicals used for farming.</li>
</ul>

<p>There are solutions to deal with these problems. A few examples include:</p><ul>
<li>Clinics offering <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/telehealth.html">telehealth</a> to provide care for people who live far away from specialists or can't easily get to their providers' offices.</li> 
<li>Local public health agencies working with their communities to promote healthy living. They can provide wellness and exercise classes and start a farmer's market.</li> 
<li>Local governments adding bike lanes and trails to encourage people to bike and walk.</li>
<li>Rural schools can offer counseling and mental health services for their students.</li>
</ul>
