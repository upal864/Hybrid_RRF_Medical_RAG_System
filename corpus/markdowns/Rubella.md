# Rubella

<p>Rubella is an infection caused by a virus. It is usually mild with fever and a rash. About half of the people who get rubella do not have symptoms. If you do get them, symptoms may include:</p><ul>
<li>A rash that starts on the face and spreads to the body</li>
<li>Mild fever</li>
<li>Aching joints, especially in young women</li>
<li>Swollen glands</li>
</ul>
 
<p>Rubella is most dangerous for a pregnant woman's baby. It can cause miscarriage or birth defects.</p>

<p>Rubella spreads when an infected person coughs or sneezes. People without symptoms can still spread it. There is no treatment, but the measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine can prevent it.</p>
