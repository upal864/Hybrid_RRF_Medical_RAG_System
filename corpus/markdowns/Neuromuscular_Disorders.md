# Neuromuscular Disorders

<p>Neuromuscular disorders affect your neuromuscular system. They can cause problems with:</p><ul>
<li>The nerves that control your muscles</li>
<li>Your muscles</li>
<li>Communication between your nerves and muscles</li>
</ul>

<p>These disorders can cause your muscles to become weak and waste away. You may also have symptoms such as spasms, twitching, and pain.</p>

<p>Examples of neuromuscular disorders include:</p><ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/amyotrophiclateralsclerosis.html">Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/musculardystrophy.html">Muscular dystrophy</a>
</li><li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/myastheniagravis.html">Myasthenia gravis</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/spinalmuscularatrophy.html">Spinal muscular atrophy</a></li>
</ul>

<p>There can be different causes for these diseases. Many of them are genetic.This means they are inherited (run in families) or are caused by a new mutation in your genes. Some neuromuscular disorders are <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/autoimmunediseases.html">autoimmune diseases</a>. Sometimes the cause is unknown.</p>

<p>Many neuromuscular diseases have no cure. But treatments may improve symptoms, increase mobility, and lengthen life.</p>
