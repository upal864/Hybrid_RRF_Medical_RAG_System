# Athlete's Foot

<p>Athlete's foot is a common infection caused by a fungus.  It most often affects the space between the toes. Symptoms include itching, burning, and cracked, scaly skin between your toes.</p>

<p>You can get athlete's foot from damp surfaces, such as showers, swimming pools, and locker room floors. To prevent it:</p><ul>
<li>Keep your feet clean, dry, and cool</li>
<li>Wear clean socks</li>
<li>Don't walk barefoot in public areas</li>
<li>Wear flip-flops in locker room showers</li>
<li>Keep your toenails clean and clipped short</li>
</ul>

<p>Treatments include over-the-counter antifungal creams for most cases and prescription medicines for more serious infections. These usually clear up the infection, but it can come back.</p>
