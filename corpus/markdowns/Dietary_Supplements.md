# Dietary Supplements

<p>Dietary supplements are <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vitamins.html">vitamins</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/minerals.html">minerals</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/herbalmedicine.html">herbs</a>, and many other products.  They can come as pills, capsules, powders, drinks, and energy bars. Supplements do not have to go through the testing that drugs do.</p>

<p>Some supplements can play an important role in health. For example, calcium and vitamin D are important for keeping bones strong. Pregnant women can take the vitamin folic acid to prevent certain birth defects in their babies.</p>

<p>To take a supplement as safely as possible:</p><ul>
<li>Tell your health care provider about any dietary supplements you use</li>
<li>Do not take a bigger dose than the label recommends</li>
<li>Check with your health care provider about the supplements you take if you are going to have any type of surgery</li>
<li>Read trustworthy information about the supplement</li>
</ul>
