# Brachial Plexus Injuries

<p>The brachial plexus is a network of nerves that sends signals from the spine to the shoulder, arm, and hand. Damage to the brachial plexus can cause symptoms such as:</p><ul>
<li>A limp or paralyzed arm</li>
<li>Lack of muscle control in the arm, hand, or wrist</li>
<li>Lack of feeling or sensation in the arm or hand</li>
</ul>

<p>Brachial plexus injuries can happen because of shoulder trauma, tumors, or inflammation. Sometimes they happen <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/childbirthproblems.html">during childbirth</a> when a baby's shoulders become stuck during delivery and the nerves stretch or tear.</p>

<p>Some brachial plexus injuries may heal without treatment. Many children who are injured during birth improve or recover by 3 to 4 months of age. Treatment includes physical therapy and, in some cases, surgery.</p>
