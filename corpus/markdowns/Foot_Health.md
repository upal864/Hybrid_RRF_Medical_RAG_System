# Foot Health

<p>Each step you take involves a complex network of bones, muscles, tendons, and ligaments. This, combined with all of the weight they carry, explains why feet can have problems. To keep your feet healthy:</p><ul>
<li>	Examine your feet regularly</li>
<li>	Wear comfortable shoes that fit</li>
<li>	Wash your feet daily with soap and lukewarm water </li>
<li>	Trim your toenails straight across and not too short</li>
</ul>

<p>Your foot health can be a clue to your overall health. For example, joint stiffness could mean arthritis. Tingling or numbness could be a sign of diabetes. Swelling might indicate kidney disease, heart disease, or high blood pressure.</p>
 
<p>Good foot care and regular foot checks are an important part of your health care. If you have foot problems, be sure to talk to your doctor. </p>
