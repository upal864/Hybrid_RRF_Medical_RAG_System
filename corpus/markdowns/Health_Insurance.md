# Health Insurance

<p>Health insurance helps protect you from high medical care costs. It is a contract between you and your insurance company. You buy a plan or policy, and the company agrees to pay part of your expenses when you need medical care.</p>

<p>Many people in the United States get a health insurance policy through their employers. In most cases, the employer helps pay for that insurance. Insurance through employers is often with a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/managedcare.html">managed care</a> plan. These plans contract with health care providers and medical facilities to provide care for members at reduced costs. You can also purchase health insurance on your own.</p>

<p>People who meet certain requirements can qualify for government health insurance, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/medicare.html">Medicare</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/medicaid.html">Medicaid</a>. The Affordable Care Act expands health insurance coverage for many people in the U.S. </p>
