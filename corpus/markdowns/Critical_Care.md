# Critical Care

<h3>What is critical care?</h3>
<p>Critical care is medical care for people who have life-threatening injuries and illnesses. It usually takes place in an intensive care unit (ICU). A team of specially-trained health care providers gives you 24-hour care. This includes using machines to constantly monitor your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vitalsigns.html">vital signs</a>. It also usually involves giving you specialized treatments.</p>

<h3>Who needs critical care?</h3>
<p>You need critical care if you have a life-threatening illness or injury, such as:</p><ul>
<li>Severe <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/burns.html">burns</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/covid19coronavirusdisease2019.html">COVID-19</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartattack.html">Heart attack</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartfailure.html">Heart failure</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/kidneyfailure.html">Kidney failure</a></li>
<li>People recovering from certain major surgeries</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/respiratoryfailure.html">Respiratory failure</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sepsis.html">Sepsis</a></li>
<li>Severe <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bleeding.html">bleeding</a></li>
<li>Serious <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/infectiousdiseases.html">infections</a></li>
<li>Serious <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/woundsandinjuries.html">injuries</a>, such as from car crashes, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/falls.html">falls</a>, and shootings</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/shock.html">Shock</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/stroke.html">Stroke</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What happens in a critical care unit?</h3>
<p>In a critical care unit, health care providers use lots of different equipment, including:</p><ul>
<li>Catheters, flexible tubes used to get fluids into the body or to drain fluids from the body</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/dialysis.html">Dialysis</a> machines ("artificial kidneys") for people with kidney failure</li>
<li>Feeding tubes, which give you <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/nutritionalsupport.html">nutritional support</a></li>
<li>Intravenous (IV) tubes to give you fluids and medicines</li>
<li>Machines which check your vital signs and display them on monitors</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/oxygentherapy.html">Oxygen therapy</a> to give you extra oxygen to breathe in</li>
<li>Tracheostomy tubes, which are breathing tubes. The tube is placed in a surgically made hole that goes through the front of the neck and into the windpipe.</li>
<li>Ventilators (breathing machines), which move air in and out of your lungs. This is for people who have respiratory failure.</li> 
</ul>

<p>These machines can help keep you alive, but many of them can also raise your risk of infection.</p>
<p>Sometimes people in a critical care unit are not able to communicate. It's important that you have an <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/advancedirectives.html">advance directive</a> in place. This can help your health care providers and family members make important decisions, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/endoflifeissues.html">end-of-life decisions</a>, if you are not able to make them.</p>
