# Tsunamis

<p>A tsunami is a series of huge ocean waves created by an underwater disturbance. Causes include <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/earthquakes.html">earthquakes</a>, landslides, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/volcanoes.html">volcanic eruptions</a>, or meteorites--chunks of rock from space that strike the surface of Earth.   A tsunami can move hundreds of miles per hour in the open ocean. It can smash into land with waves as high as 100 feet or more and cause devastating <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/floods.html">floods</a>. <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/drowning.html">Drowning</a> is the most common cause of death related to a tsunami.</p>

<p>Although there are no guarantees of safety during a tsunami, you can take actions to protect yourself.  You should have a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/disasterpreparationandrecovery.html">disaster plan</a>. Being prepared can help reduce fear, anxiety, and losses. If you do experience a disaster, it is normal to feel stressed. You may need help in finding <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/copingwithdisasters.html">ways to cope</a>.</p>
