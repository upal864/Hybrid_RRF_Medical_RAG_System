# Suicide

<h3>What is suicide? </h3>
<p>Suicide is the taking of one's own life. It is a death that happens when someone harms themselves because they want to end their life. A suicide attempt is when someone harms themselves to try to end their life, but they do not die.</p>
<p>Suicide is a major public health problem and a leading cause of death in the United States. Both suicide and suicide attempts can have lasting harmful effects:</p>
<ul>
<li>People who survive a suicide attempt may have serious injuries that can affect their long-term health. They may also have <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/depression.html">depression</a> or other mental health concerns.</li>
<li>When people die by suicide, it affects their family, friends, and community. They may feel grief, shock, anger, and guilt. Some may develop <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/posttraumaticstressdisorder.html">post-traumatic stress disorder</a> (PTSD), depression, or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/anxiety.html">anxiety</a>. They may also be at risk for suicidal thoughts.</li>
</ul>


<h3>Who is at risk for suicide?</h3>
<p>Suicide does not discriminate. It can touch anyone, anywhere, at any time. But there are certain factors that can contribute to the risk of suicide, including:</p><ul>
<li>Having attempted suicide before</li>
<li>Depression and other <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/mentaldisorders.html">mental health disorders</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/alcoholusedisorderaud.html">Alcohol</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/druguseandaddiction.html">drug</a> use disorder</li>
<li>Family history of a mental health disorder</li>
<li>Family history of an alcohol or drug use disorder</li>
<li>Family history of suicide</li>
<li>Family violence, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/intimatepartnerviolence.html">intimate partner violence</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/childabuse.html">child abuse</a>, or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sexualassault.html">sexual</a> abuse</li>
<li>Having guns in the home</li>
<li>Being in or having recently gotten out of prison or jail</li>
<li>Being exposed to others' suicidal behavior, such as a family member, peer, or celebrity</li>
<li>Medical illness, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/chronicpain.html">chronic pain</a></li>
<li>Stressful life event, such as a job loss, financial problems, loss of a loved one, a breakup of a relationship, etc.</li>
<li>Being between the ages of 15 and 24 years or over age 60</li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the warning signs for suicide?</h3>
<p>The warning signs for suicide include:</p><ul>
<li>Talking about wanting to die or wanting to kill oneself</li>
<li>Making a plan or looking for a way to kill oneself, such as searching online</li>
<li>Buying a gun or stockpiling pills</li>
<li>Feeling empty, hopeless, trapped, or like there's no reason to live</li>
<li>Being in unbearable pain</li>
<li>Talking about being a burden to others</li>
<li>Using more alcohol or drugs</li>
<li>Acting anxious or agitated; behaving recklessly</li>
<li>Sleeping too little or too much</li>
<li>Withdrawing from family or friends or feeling isolated</li>
<li>Showing rage or talking about seeking revenge</li>
<li>Displaying extreme mood swings</li>
<li>Saying good-bye to loved ones, putting affairs in order</li>
</ul>

<p>Some people may tell others about their suicidal thoughts. But others may try to hide them. This can make some of the signs harder to spot.</p>

<h3>What should I do if I need help or know someone who does?</h3>
<p>If you or someone you know has the warning signs for suicide, <strong>get help right away</strong>, especially if there is a change in behavior. If it is an emergency, dial 911. Otherwise there are five steps that you can take:</p><ul>
<li><strong>Ask</strong> the person if they're thinking about killing themselves.</li>
<li><strong>Keep them safe.</strong> Find out whether they have a plan for suicide and keep them away from things that they can use to kill themselves.</li>
<li><strong>Be there with them.</strong> Listen carefully and find out what they are thinking and feeling.</li>
<li><strong>Help them connect</strong> to resources that can help them, such as through:
		<ul>
			<li>Calling or texting the 988 Suicide &amp; Crisis Lifeline at 988.</li>
                        <li>Chatting through <a href="https://988lifeline.org/chat/">Lifeline Chat.</a></li>
			<li>For veterans, reaching the Veterans Crisis Line by: 
                              <ul>
                              <li>Calling 988 and then pressing 1.</li>
                              <li>Texting to 838255.</li>
                              <li><a href="https://www.veteranscrisisline.net/get-help-now/chat/">Chatting</a> with them.</li>
                              </ul> 
                        </li>                    
		</ul>
</li>		
<li><strong>Stay connected.</strong> Staying in touch after a crisis can make a difference.</li>
</ul>
