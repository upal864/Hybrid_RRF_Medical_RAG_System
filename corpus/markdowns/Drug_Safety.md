# Drug Safety

<p>Before any drug can be sold in the United States, it must be approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). This is true whether it's a prescription or an <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/overthecountermedicines.html">over-the-counter</a> drug. The FDA evaluates both the effectiveness and safety of a drug by looking at: </p>
<ul>
<li>How animal testing and human clinical trials affect the condition the drug is treating</li>
<li>If any <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/drugreactions.html">side effects</a> occur</li>
<li>How it's manufactured</li>
<li>What the labeling says</li>
</ul>

<p>The FDA also monitors a drug's safety after approval. Health care providers and patients can report drug side effects through the FDA's <a href="https://www.fda.gov/safety/medwatch-fda-safety-information-and-adverse-event-reporting-program">MedWatch website</a>.</p>

<p>For you, drug safety means buying online only from licensed pharmacies located in the United States. It also means knowing how to <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/medicationerrors.html">take your medicines correctly</a>.</p>
