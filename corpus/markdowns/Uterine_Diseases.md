# Uterine Diseases

<h3>What are uterine diseases?</h3>
<p>The uterus, or womb, is the place where a fetus grows during pregnancy. Uterine diseases are conditions that affect the uterus. These diseases can be benign (not cancer) or malignant (<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/uterinecancer.html">cancerous</a>). They may affect your menstrual periods, your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/femaleinfertility.html">ability to get pregnant</a>, or the health of your reproductive organs.</p> 

<p>Some types of uterine diseases include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Adenomyosis.</strong> Tissue that usually lines the uterus grows on the outside walls.</li>
<li><strong>Cervicitis.</strong> Swelling or inflamed tissue of the cervix. The cervix is the lower part of the uterus.</li> 
<li><strong>Endometrial hyperplasia.</strong> An overgrowth of normal cells inside the uterus.</li>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/endometriosis.html">Endometriosis</a>.</strong> Tissue like the lining of the uterus grows in other places in your body.</li>
<li><strong>Uterine prolapse.</strong> The uterus drops in to the vagina due to weakened pelvic muscles. This can happen after a few vaginal births, menopause, or because of obesity.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What causes uterine diseases?</h3>
<p>Various factors can cause uterine diseases. These can include hormonal imbalances, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/thyroiddiseases.html">thyroid problems</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/uterinefibroids.html">fibroids</a>, polyps, cancer, infection, or pregnancy.</p> 

<h3>What are the symptoms of uterine diseases?</h3>
<p>Your symptoms will depend on the type of uterine condition you have. The first sign of a problem with the uterus may be abnormal <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vaginalbleeding.html">bleeding</a>. Bleeding could occur between periods, be very heavy, or last much longer than usual. Other symptoms may include bleeding after sex or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pelvicpain.html">pelvic pain</a>.</p>

<h3>How are uterine diseases diagnosed?</h3>
<p>To find out the cause of your symptoms, your health care provider may:</p>
<ul>
<li>Ask about your symptoms and medical history</li>
<li>Do a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/womenshealthcheckup.html">pelvic exam</a></li>
<li>Order blood tests or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diagnosticimaging.html">imaging tests</a></li> 
<li>Do a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/biopsy.html">biopsy</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the treatments for uterine diseases?</h3>
<p>Treatment depends on the cause. It may include pain medicine, hormone therapy, or surgery. Your provider will work with you to choose the best treatment.</p>
