# Male Breast Cancer

<p>Although breast cancer is much more common in women, men can get it too. It happens most often to men between the ages of 60 and 70.</p>

<p>Breast lumps usually aren't cancer. However, most men with breast cancer have lumps. Other breast symptoms can include:</p><ul>
<li>Dimpled or puckered skin</li>
<li>A red, scaly nipple or skin</li>
<li>Fluid discharge</li>
</ul>

<p>Risk factors for male breast cancer include exposure to radiation, a family history of breast cancer, and having high estrogen levels, which can happen with diseases like cirrhosis or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/klinefeltersyndrome.html">Klinefelter syndrome</a>.</p>

<p>Treatment for male breast cancer is usually a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/mastectomy.html">mastectomy</a>, which is surgery to remove the breast. Other treatments include radiation, chemotherapy and/or hormone therapy. </p>
