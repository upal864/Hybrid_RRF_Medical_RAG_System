# Vitamin D

<h3> Why do I need vitamin D?</h3>
<p><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vitamins.html">Vitamins</a> are substances that your body needs to grow and develop normally. Vitamin D is important to your body because:</p>
<ul>
<li>It helps your body absorb <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/calcium.html">calcium</a>. Calcium is one of the main building blocks of bone. <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vitaminddeficiency.html">A lack of vitamin D</a> can lead to bone diseases such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/osteoporosis.html">osteoporosis</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/rickets.html">rickets</a>.</li>
<li>It also has a role in your nerve, muscle, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/immunesystemanddisorders.html">immune</a> systems:
  <ul>
  <li>Your muscles need it to move</li>
  <li>Your nerves need it to carry messages between your brain and your body</li>
  <li>Your immune system needs vitamin D to fight off bacteria and viruses</li>
  </ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h3>How do I get vitamin D?</h3>
<p>You can get vitamin D in three ways: through your skin, from your diet, and from supplements. Your body forms vitamin D naturally after <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sunexposure.html">exposure to sunlight</a>. However, too much sun exposure can lead to <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/skinaging.html">skin aging</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/skincancer.html">skin cancer</a>. So many people try to get their vitamin D from other sources.</p>

<p>Very few foods naturally contain vitamin D. Most people get vitamin D in their diet from foods that are fortified. This means that vitamin D is added to the food. These foods may include milk, cereal, and yogurt. Foods that naturally have vitamin D include egg yolks, saltwater fish, and liver. </p>

<p>Vitamin D is available in supplements. It is also included in many multivitamins. The two forms of vitamin D in supplements are D2 and D3. Both types increase vitamin D in your blood, but D3 might raise it higher and for longer than D2. Vitamin D is fat soluble. You can absorb it better when you take your supplements with a meal or snack that includes some fat.</p>

<h3>How much vitamin D do I need?</h3>
<p>The amount of vitamin D you need each day depends on your age. The recommended amounts, in international units (IU), are:</p><ul>
<li>Birth to 12 months: 400 IU</li>
<li>Children 1-13 years: 600 IU</li>
<li>Teens 14-18 years: 600 IU</li>
<li>Adults 19-70 years: 600 IU</li>
<li>Adults 71 years and older: 800 IU</li>
<li>If you are pregnant or breastfeeding: 600 IU</li>
</ul>

<p>But certain people may need extra vitamin D, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>Older adults</li>
<li>Breastfed infants</li>
<li>People with dark skin</li>
<li>People with  certain conditions that limit the absorption of fat, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/crohnsdisease.html">Crohn's disease</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/celiacdisease.html">celiac disease</a>, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/ulcerativecolitis.html">ulcerative colitis</a></li>
<li>People who have <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/obesity.html">obesity</a> or have had <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/weightlosssurgery.html">gastric bypass surgery</a></li>
</ul>

<p>Check with your health care provider to see if you need to take vitamin D supplements, and if so, how much you should take. Your provider may want to first do a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/lab-tests/vitamin-d-test/">vitamin D test </a> to see if you are getting enough of it.</p>
