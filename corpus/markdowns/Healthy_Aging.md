# Healthy Aging

<p>People in the U.S. are living longer, and the number of older adults in the population is growing. As we age, our minds and bodies change. Having a healthy lifestyle can help you deal with those changes. It may also prevent some health problems and  help you to make the most of your life.</p>
<p>A healthy lifestyle for older adults includes:</p><ul>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/nutritionforolderadults.html">Healthy eating</a>.</strong> As you age, your dietary needs may change. You may need fewer calories, but you still need to get enough nutrients. A healthy eating plan includes 
	<ul>
		<li>Eating foods that give you lots of nutrients without a lot of extra calories. This includes fruits and vegetables, whole grains, lean meats, low-fat dairy, nuts, and seeds.</li>
		<li>Avoiding empty calories, such as foods like chips, candy, baked goods, soda, and alcohol</li>
		<li>Eating foods that are low in cholesterol and fat</li>
		<li>Drinking enough liquids, so you don't get <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/dehydration.html">dehydrated</a></li>
	</ul>
</li>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/exerciseforolderadults.html">Regular physical activity</a>.</strong> Being physically active may help you maintain a healthy weight and avoid chronic health problems. If you have not been active, you can start slowly and work up to your goal. <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/howmuchexercisedoineed.html">How much exercise you need</a> depends on your age and health. Check with your health care provider on what is right for you.</li> 
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/weightcontrol.html">Staying at a healthy weight</a>.</strong> Being either overweight or underweight can lead to health problems. Ask your health care professional what a healthy weight for you may be. Healthy eating and exercise can help you get to that weight.</li>
<li><strong>Keeping your mind active.</strong> Lots of activities can keep your mind active and improve your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/memory.html">memory</a>, including learning new skills, reading, and playing games.</li>
<li><strong>Making your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/mentalhealth.html">mental health</a> a priority.</strong> Work on <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/howtoimprovementalhealth.html">improving your mental health</a>, for example by practicing mediation, relaxation techniques, or gratitude. Know the warning signs of a problem and ask for help if you are struggling.</li> 
<li><strong>Participating in activities that you enjoy.</strong> People who are involved in hobbies and social and leisure activities may be at lower risk for some health problems. Doing things that you enjoy may help you feel happier and improve your thinking abilities.</li>
<li><strong>Playing an active role in your health care.</strong> Make sure that you get <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/healthcheckup.html">regular checkups</a> and the <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/healthscreening.html">health screenings</a> that you need. You should know which medicines you are taking, why you need them, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/medicationerrors.html">how to take them properly</a>.</li>
<li><strong>Not smoking.</strong> If you are a smoker, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/quittingsmoking.html">quitting</a> is one of the most important things that you can do for your health. It can lower your risk of several different types of cancer, certain lung diseases, and heart disease.</li>
<li><strong>Taking steps to prevent <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/falls.html">falls</a>.</strong> Older adults have a higher risk of falling. They are also more likely to <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fractures.html">fracture</a> (break) a bone when they fall. Getting regular <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/eyecare.html">eye checkups</a>, getting regular physical activity, and making your house safer can lower your risk of falling.</li>
</ul>

<p>Following these tips can help you to stay healthy as you age. Even if you have never done them before, it's never too late to start taking care of your health. If you have questions about these lifestyle changes or need help figuring out how to make them, ask your health care provider.</p>
