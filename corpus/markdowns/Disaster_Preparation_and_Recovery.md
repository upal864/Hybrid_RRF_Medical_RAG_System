# Disaster Preparation and Recovery


<p>Preparing for a disaster can reduce the fear, anxiety and losses that disasters cause. A disaster can be a  natural disaster, like a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hurricanes.html">hurricane</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/tornadoes.html">tornado</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/floods.html">flood</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/earthquakes.html">earthquake</a>. It might also be  man-made, like a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/biodefenseandbioterrorism.html">bioterrorist attack</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/chemicalemergencies.html">chemical spill</a>. You should know the risks and danger signs of different types of disasters. You should also have a disaster plan. Be ready to evacuate your home, and know how to treat basic medical problems. Make sure you have the insurance you need, including special types, like flood insurance.</p>

<p>No matter what kind of disaster you experience, it causes <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/copingwithdisasters.html">emotional distress</a>. After a disaster, recovery can take time. Stay connected to your family and friends during this period.</p>
