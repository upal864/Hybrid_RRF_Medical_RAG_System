# Safety

<p>You can't remove all the safety hazards from your life, but you can reduce them. To avoid many major hazards and prepare for emergencies:</p><ul>
<li>	Keep emergency phone numbers by your telephones</li>
<li>	Make a first aid kit for your home</li>
<li>	Make a family emergency plan</li>
<li>	Install and maintain <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/firesafety.html">smoke</a> alarms and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/carbonmonoxidepoisoning.html">carbon monoxide</a> detectors</li>
<li>	Keep guns unloaded and locked up. Lock up the ammunition separately. </li>
<li>	Follow the directions carefully when using tools or equipment</li>
</ul>

<p>Young <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/childsafety.html">children</a> are especially at risk.  Supervision is the best way to keep them safe. Childproofing the house can also help.</p>
