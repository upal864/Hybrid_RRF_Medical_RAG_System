# Child Nutrition

<p>A healthy diet helps children grow and learn. It also helps prevent <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/obesityinchildren.html">obesity</a> and weight-related diseases, such as diabetes. To give your child a nutritious diet:</p><ul>
<li>Make half of what is on your child's plate fruits and vegetables</li>
<li>Choose healthy sources of protein, such as lean meat, nuts, and eggs</li>
<li>Serve whole-grain breads and cereals because they are high in fiber. Reduce refined grains.</li>
<li>Broil, grill, or steam foods instead of frying them</li>
<li>Limit fast food and junk food</li>
<li>Offer water or milk instead of sugary fruit drinks and sodas</li>
</ul>

<p>Learn about your children's nutrient requirements. Some of them, such as the requirements for iron and calcium, change as your child ages. </p>
