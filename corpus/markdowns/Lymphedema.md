# Lymphedema

<p>Lymphedema is the name of a type of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/edema.html">swelling</a>. It happens when lymph builds up in your body's soft tissues.  Lymph is a fluid that contains white blood cells that defend against germs. It can build up when the <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/lymphaticdiseases.html">lymph system</a> is damaged or blocked. It usually happens in the arms or legs.</p>

<p>Causes of lymphedema include:</p><ul>
<li>Infection</li>
<li>Cancer</li>
<li>Scar tissue from radiation therapy or surgical removal of lymph nodes</li>
<li>Inherited conditions in which lymph nodes or vessels are absent or abnormal</li>
</ul>

<p>Treatment can help control symptoms.  It includes exercise, compression devices, skin care, and massage.</p>
