# Anabolic Steroids

<h3>What are anabolic steroids?</h3> 
<p>Anabolic steroids are synthetic (man-made) versions of testosterone. Testosterone is the main sex <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hormones.html">hormone</a> in men. It is needed to develop and maintain male sex characteristics, such as facial hair, deep voice, and muscle growth. Women do have some testosterone in their bodies, but in much smaller amounts.</p>

<h3>What are anabolic steroids used for?</h3>
<p>Health care providers use anabolic steroids to treat some hormone problems in men, delayed <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/puberty.html">puberty</a>, and muscle loss from some diseases. But some people misuse anabolic steroids by taking them in a way that's different than their provider prescribed.</p>  

<h3>Why do people misuse anabolic steroids?</h3>
<p>Some bodybuilders and athletes use anabolic steroids to build muscles and improve athletic performance. They may take steroids by swallowing tablets or capsules, inject them into muscles, or apply them to the skin as a gel or cream. These doses may be 10 to 100 times higher than doses used to treat medical conditions. Using them this way, without a prescription from a provider, is not legal or safe.</p> 

<h3>What are the health effects of misusing anabolic steroids?</h3>
<p>Misuse of anabolic steroids, especially over a long period of time, has been linked with many health problems, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>Acne</li>
<li>Stunted growth in teens</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/highbloodpressure.html">High blood pressure</a></li>
<li>Changes in <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cholesterol.html">cholesterol</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartdiseases.html">Heart problems</a>, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartattack.html">heart attack</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/liverdiseases.html">Liver disease</a>, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/livercancer.html">cancer</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/kidneydiseases.html">Kidney damage</a></li>
<li>Mood changes such as irritability and aggressive behavior</li>
</ul>

<p>In men, it can also cause:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hairloss.html">Baldness</a></li>
<li>Breast growth</li>
<li>Low sperm count/<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/maleinfertility.html">infertility</a></li>
<li>Shrinking of the testicles</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/testicularcancer.html">Testicular cancer</a></li>
</ul>

<p>In women, it can also cause:</p>
<ul>
<li>Changes in your <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/menstruation.html">menstrual cycle</a> (period)</li>
<li>Growth of body and facial hair</li>
<li>Male-pattern baldness</li>
<li>Voice deepening</li>
<li>Decreased breast size</li>
</ul>

<h3>Are anabolic steroids addictive?</h3>
<p>Even though they don't cause a high, anabolic steroids can lead to addiction. You can have withdrawal symptoms if you stop using them, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fatigue.html">Fatigue</a></li>
<li>Restlessness</li>
<li>Loss of appetite</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sleepdisorders.html">Sleep problems</a></li>
<li>Decreased sex drive</li>
<li>Steroid cravings</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/depression.html">Depression</a>, which can sometimes be serious and even lead to <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/suicide.html">suicide</a> attempts</li>
</ul>

<p>Behavioral therapy and medicines can be helpful in treating anabolic steroid addiction</p>
