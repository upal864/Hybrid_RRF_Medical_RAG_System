# B Vitamins

<p>The B vitamins are:</p><ul>
<li>B1 (thiamine)</li>
<li>B2 (riboflavin)</li>
<li>B3 (niacin)</li>
<li>B5 (pantothenic acid)</li>
<li>B6</li>
<li>	B7 (biotin)</li>
<li>B12</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/folicacid.html">Folic acid</a></li>
</ul>

<p>These vitamins help the process your body uses to get or make energy from the food you eat. They also help form red blood cells.  You can get B vitamins from proteins such as fish, poultry, meat, eggs, and dairy products.  Leafy green vegetables, beans, and peas also have B vitamins.  Many cereals and some breads have added B vitamins.</p>

<p>Not getting enough of certain B vitamins can cause diseases.  A lack of B12 or B6 can cause <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/anemia.html">anemia</a>.</p>
