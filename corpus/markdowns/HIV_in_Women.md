# HIV in Women

<h3>What is HIV?</h3>
<p><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hiv.html">HIV</a> stands for human immunodeficiency virus. It harms your immune system by destroying a type of white blood cell that helps your body fight infection. This puts you at risk for other infections and diseases.</p>

<h3>How does HIV spread?</h3>
<p>HIV can spread in different ways:</p>
<ul>
<li>By having unprotected vaginal or anal sex with a person who has HIV. "Unprotected" means not using condoms or medicine to treat or prevent HIV. This is the most common way it spreads. 
	<ul>
		<li>Anal sex is the riskiest type of sex for getting or spreading HIV. The rectum's lining is thin and may allow HIV to enter the body during anal sex.</li>
		<li>During vaginal sex, HIV can enter the body through the delicate tissue that lines the vagina and cervix.</li>
	</ul>
</li>
<li>Rarely, through oral sex with a person who has HIV.</li>
<li>By sharing drug needles.</li>
<li>Through contact with the blood of a person who has HIV.</li>
<li>From mother to fetus during <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hivandpregnancy.html">pregnancy</a>.</li>
<li>From mother to baby during childbirth, or breastfeeding.</li>
</ul>

<h3>How does HIV affect women differently from men?</h3>
<p>About one in four people in the United States who have HIV are women. Women who have HIV have some different problems than men:</p>
<ul>
<li>Complications such as
	<ul> 
		<li>Repeated <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/yeastinfections.html">vaginal yeast infections</a></li>
		<li>Bacterial vaginosis</li>
		<li>Severe <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pelvicinflammatorydisease.html">pelvic inflammatory disease</a> (PID)</li>
		<li>A higher risk of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cervicalcancer.html">cervical cancer</a></li>
		<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/menstruation.html">Menstrual cycle problems</a></li>
		<li>A higher risk of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/osteoporosis.html">osteoporosis</a></li>
		<li>A higher risk of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartdiseaseinwomen.html">heart disease</a>, especially <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartattack.html">heart attacks</a></li>
		<li>Entering <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/menopause.html">menopause</a> younger or having more severe hot flashes</li>
	</ul>
</li>
<li>Different, sometimes more severe, side effects from the medicines that treat HIV</li>
<li>Drug interactions between some <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hivmedicines.html">HIV medicines</a> and hormonal <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/birthcontrol.html">birth control</a></li>
<li>The risk of giving HIV to their fetus while pregnant</li>
<li>The risk of giving HIV to their baby during childbirth or breastfeeding</li>
</ul>

<h3>Are there treatments for HIV?</h3>
<p>There is no cure, but there are many medicines to treat both HIV infection and the infections and cancers that come with it. People who get early treatment can live <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/livingwithhiv.html">longer and healthier lives</a>.</p>
