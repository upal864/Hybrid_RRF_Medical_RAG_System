# Colonic Diseases

<p>Your colon, also known as the large intestine, is part of your digestive system. It's a long, hollow tube at the end of your digestive tract where your body makes and stores stool. Many disorders affect the colon's ability to work properly. Some of these include:</p><ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/colorectalcancer.html">Colorectal cancer</a> </li>
<li>	<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/colonicpolyps.html">Colonic polyps</a> - extra tissue growing in the colon that can become cancerous</li>
<li>	<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/ulcerativecolitis.html">Ulcerative colitis</a> - ulcers of the colon and rectum</li>
<li>	<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diverticulosisanddiverticulitis.html">Diverticulitis</a> - inflammation or infection of pouches in the colon</li>
<li>	<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/irritablebowelsyndrome.html">Irritable bowel syndrome</a>  -  an uncomfortable condition causing abdominal cramping and other symptoms</li>
</ul>
<p>Treatment for colonic diseases varies greatly depending on the disease and its severity. Treatment may involve diet, medicines and in some cases, surgery.</p>
