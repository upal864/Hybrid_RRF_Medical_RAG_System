# Urinary Tract Infections

<p>The urinary system is the body's drainage system for removing wastes and extra water. It includes two kidneys, two ureters, a bladder, and a urethra. Urinary tract infections (UTIs) are the second most common type of infection in the body.</p>

<p>You may have a UTI if you notice:</p><ul>
<li>Pain or burning when you urinate</li>
<li>Fever, tiredness, or shakiness</li>
<li>An urge to urinate often</li>
<li>Pressure in your lower belly </li>
<li>Urine that smells bad or looks cloudy or reddish</li>
<li>Pain in your back or side below the ribs</li>
</ul>

<p>People of any age or sex can get UTIs. But about four times as many women get UTIs as men. You're also at higher risk if you have diabetes, need a tube to drain your bladder, or have a spinal cord injury.</p>

<p>If you think you have a UTI it is important to see your doctor. Your doctor can tell if you have a UTI with a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/urinalysis.html">urine test</a>.  Treatment is with antibiotics. </p>
