# Winter Weather Emergencies

<h3>What kinds of problems can severe winter weather cause?</h3>
<p>Winter storms can bring extreme cold, freezing rain, snow, ice, and high winds. Staying safe and warm can be a challenge. You may have to cope with problems such as:</p><ul>
<li>Cold-related health problems, including <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/frostbite.html">frostbite</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hypothermia.html">hypothermia</a></li>
<li>Household fires and carbon monoxide poisoning from space heaters and fireplaces</li>
<li>Unsafe driving conditions on icy roads</li>
<li>Power failures and loss of communication</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/floods.html">Floods</a> after the snow and ice melt</li>
</ul>

<h3>How can I prepare for a winter weather emergency?</h3>
If there is a winter storm coming, there are things you can do to try to keep yourself and your loved ones safe:  
<ul>
<li>Have a <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/disasterpreparationandrecovery.html">disaster plan</a> that includes</li>
       <ul>
	<li>Making sure that you have important phone numbers, including numbers for your health care providers, pharmacy, and veterinarian</li>
	<li>Having a communication plan for your family</li>
	<li>Knowing how to get reliable information during the storm</li>
	</ul>
<li>Prepare your home to keep out the cold with insulation, caulking, and weather stripping. Learn how to keep pipes from freezing.</li> 
<li>Gather supplies in case you need to stay home for several days without power</li>
<li>If you plan to use your fireplace or wood stove for emergency heating, have your chimney or flue inspected each year</li>
<li>Install a smoke detector and a battery-operated carbon monoxide detector</li>
<li>If you have to travel, be sure you have an emergency car kit with some basic supplies like</li>
		<ul>		
			<li>An ice scraper</li>
		 	<li>A shovel</li>
			<li>Cat litter or sand for better tire traction </li>
			<li>Water and snacks</li>
			<li>Extra warm clothing</li>
			<li>Jumper cables</li>
			<li>First aid kit with any necessary medicines and a pocketknife</li>
			<li>A battery-powered radio, a flashlight, and extra batteries</li>
			<li>Emergency flares or distress flags</li>
			<li>Waterproof matches and a can to melt snow for water</li>
		</ul>	
</ul>

<p>If you do experience a disaster, it is normal to feel stressed. You may need help in finding <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/copingwithdisasters.html">ways to cope</a>.</p>
