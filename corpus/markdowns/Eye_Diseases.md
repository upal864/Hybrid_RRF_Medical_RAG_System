# Eye Diseases

<h3>What diseases can affect the eye?</h3> 
<p>Even if your eyes feel healthy, you could have a problem and not know it. That's why regular <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/eyecare.html">eye exams</a> are so important. <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/refractiveerrors.html">Refractive errors</a> are the most common type of vision problem that makes it hard to see clearly. But some eye conditions or diseases don't have any symptoms and can lead to a permanent <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/visionimpairmentandblindness.html">loss of vision</a>.</p> 

<p>Some common eye diseases and conditions include:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cataract.html">Cataracts</a></strong>, which happens when the clear lens in the front of your eye becomes cloudy. Cataracts are common as you get older and can lead to vision loss.</li>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/opticnervedisorders.html">Optic nerve disorders</a></strong>, which includes <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/glaucoma.html">glaucoma</a>. Glaucoma usually happens when the fluid pressure inside your eyes slowly rises.</li>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/retinaldisorders.html">Retinal disorders</a></strong>, which are problems with the nerve layer at the back of your eye.</li>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/maculardegeneration.html">Macular degeneration</a></strong>, which is a disease that destroys your sharp, central vision.</li>
<li><strong><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabeticeyeproblems.html">Diabetic eye problems</a></strong>, which can cause vision loss and blindness.</li>
<li><strong>Conjunctivitis</strong>, which is an infection also known as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pinkeye.html">pink eye</a>. It can cause pink or red itchy eyes. Some types of pink eye can spread very easily.</li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the symptoms of eye diseases?</h3>
<p>Some eye conditions and diseases do not always have symptoms. Early detection through a comprehensive dilated eye exam could help your eye care provider detect diseases in the early stages. Proper treatment could help slow or prevent vision loss.</p> 

<p>Get regular eye exams and call your eye care professional right away if you notice any of these symptoms:</p>
<ul>
<li>A sudden change in vision</li> 
<li>Everything looks dim</li>
<li>Flashes of light</li> 
<li>Eye pain</li>
<li>Double vision</li>
<li>Fluid coming from your eye</li>
<li>Inflammation (swelling)</li>
</ul>

<h3>Who is more likely to develop eye diseases?</h3>
<p>Getting older increases your risk of some eye diseases. Other things that may put you at a higher risk of some eye diseases can include if you:</p>
<ul>
<li>Are overweight or obese</li>
<li>Have a family history of eye disease</li>
<li>Are African American, Hispanic, or Native American</li>
<li>Have certain health conditions such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabeticeyeproblems.html">Diabetic eye problems</a>, or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/howtopreventhighbloodpressure.html">high blood pressure</a></li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the treatments for eye diseases?</h3>
<p>Treatment may depend on the eye condition or disease but can include:</p>
<ul>
<li>A new prescription for <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/eyewear.html">eyeglasses or contacts</a></li>
<li>Use of bright lights or magnifying lenses for reading and other activities</li>
<li>Medicines</li> 
<li>Prescription eye drops or ointments</li>
<li>Laser treatment</li>
<li>Surgery</li>
</ul>

<p>Depending on your eye condition or disease, treatment may slow vision loss but may not restore vision. Talk to your provider about what treatment is best for you.</p>

<h3>Can eye diseases be prevented?</h3>
<p>Your best defense is to have regular checkups. Be sure to tell your provider if any eye diseases run in your family.</p> 

<p>You can also help take care of your vision by making lifestyle changes to lower your risk of health conditions that can cause vision problems. This could include by:</p>
<ul>
<li>Protecting your eyes from the sun</li> 
<li>Wearing protective eyewear while using tools or playing certain sports</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/howmuchexercisedoineed.html">Getting regular physical activity</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/weightcontrol.html">Staying at a healthy weight</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/quittingsmoking.html">Quitting smoking</a> (or not starting)</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/nutrition.html">Eating a healthy diet</a></li>
</ul>
