# Intestinal Obstruction

<p>An intestinal obstruction occurs when food or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/bowelmovement.html">stool</a> cannot move through the intestines. The obstruction can be complete or partial. There are many causes. The most common are <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/adhesions.html">adhesions</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hernia.html">hernias</a>, cancers, and certain medicines. </p>
<p>Symptoms include:</p><ul>
<li>Severe abdominal pain or cramping </li>
<li>Vomiting </li>
<li>Bloating </li>
<li>Loud bowel sounds </li>
<li>Swelling of the abdomen </li>
<li>Inability to pass gas </li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/constipation.html">Constipation</a></li>
</ul>
<p>A complete intestinal obstruction is a medical emergency.  It often requires surgery.</p>
