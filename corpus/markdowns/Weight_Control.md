# Weight Control

<h3>Why is weight control important?</h3>
<p>If you are struggling with your weight, you are not alone. In the United States, more than 70 percent of adults are overweight or have <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/obesity.html">obesity</a>. Having this extra weight raises your risk for many health conditions, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/diabetestype2.html">type 2 diabetes</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/heartdiseases.html">heart disease</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/kidneydiseases.html">kidney disease</a>, and certain cancers.</p>

<p>Reaching and staying at a healthy weight can be challenging. But having a healthy lifestyle, including healthy eating patterns and regular physical activity, can help you lose weight. It can also lower your chance of developing weight-related health conditions.</p>      

<h3>What factors affect weight and health?</h3>
<p>You gain weight when you take in more calories (through food and drinks) than you use up from physical activity and daily living. But there are many different factors that can affect weight gain, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>The world around you.</strong> Your home, community, and workplace all may affect how you make daily lifestyle choices. For example:
	<ul>
		<li>It is often easier to find food and beverages high in calories, sugar, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/dietaryfats.html">fat</a>. For instance, vending machines, cafeterias, and special events may not offer healthy, lower calorie options.</li> 
		<li>Less healthy foods may be cheaper than healthier foods.</li>  
		<li>Many people are getting less physical activity because they are spending more time using smartphones and other devices.</li> 
	</ul>
</li>	
<li><strong>Families.</strong> Overweight and obesity tend to run in families. This suggests that genes may play a role in weight gain. Families may also share eating and lifestyle habits. For example, some families may often have foods and drinks that are high in calories, sugar, and fat. And some families may tend to be less active and spend more time doing things like sitting and watching TV or using computers.</li>
<li><strong>Not enough sleep.</strong> People who don't get enough sleep may eat more calories and snack more.</li>
<li><strong>Emotions.</strong> Some people eat when they feel bored, sad, or stressed, even if they are not hungry.</li> 
<li><strong>Medicines and health conditions.</strong> Taking certain medicines, such as <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/steroids.html">steroids</a> and certain <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/antidepressants.html">antidepressants</a>, can lead to weight gain. Some chronic health problems can also cause you to gain weight. A few examples are <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cushingssyndrome.html">Cushing's syndrome</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/polycysticovarysyndrome.html">polycystic ovary syndrome</a> (PCOS).</li>
</ul>

<h3>How can I get to and stay at a healthy weight?</h3>
<p>Getting to and staying at a healthy weight involves finding a balance of food and activity. To lose weight, you need to take in fewer calories than you use up. Some ways to do this are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Eating more nutrient-rich foods, such as foods with lots of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/vitamins.html">vitamins</a>, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/minerals.html">minerals</a>, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/dietaryfiber.html">fiber</a>.</li> 
<li>Eating and drinking less of the foods and beverages that have lots of calories, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sodium.html">salt</a>, sugar, and fat.</li> 
<li>Limiting <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/alcohol.html">alcohol</a>.</li> 
<li>Finding healthier ways to cook, such as using healthier oils to cook with and baking or grilling instead of frying foods.</li> 
<li>Getting more <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/howmuchexercisedoineed.html">physical activity</a>. The general recommendation is for adults to get 150 minutes of physical activity each week. This time can be broken down into short bursts of activity throughout your day. Types of physical activity include: 
	<ul>
		<li>Aerobic activity, which is also called cardio. It uses your large muscle groups (chest, legs, and back) to speed up your heart rate and breathing.</li> 
		<li>Muscle-strengthening activity, which is also called strength training. It works your muscles by making you push or pull against something.</li> 
	</ul>
</li> 
</ul>

<p>You may decide to do these lifestyle changes on your own, or you may decide to try a weight-loss diet or program. Before you start, it's important to check with your health care provider first. Your provider can tell you what a healthy weight is for you, help you set goals, and give you tips on how to lose weight.</p> 

<h3>What are weight management medicines?</h3>
<p>If making lifestyle changes or doing a weight-loss program are not enough to help you lose weight, your provider may prescribe medicines. These medicines are meant to help people who have weight-related health conditions and should be used along with healthy eating and physical activity. The prescription medicines to treat overweight and obesity work in different ways. Some may help you feel less hungry or full sooner. Others may make it harder for your body to absorb fat from the foods you eat.</p>

<p>Another treatment is <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/weightlosssurgery.html">weight loss surgery</a>. Your provider may recommend the surgery if you have severe obesity or serious obesity-related health problems and you have not been able to lose enough weight.</p>

<h3>How can I maintain my weight loss?</h3>
<p>Maintaining weight loss can be challenging, and it's normal to slip back into old habits sometimes. The key is to keep a healthy balance of food and activity that works for you. Checking your weight regularly can help you notice small changes early and stay on track. And if you have a setback, don't be discouraged, simply refocus on your healthy habits and keep going.</p>
