# Birth Control

<h3>What is birth control?</h3>
<p>Birth control, also known as contraception, is the use of medicines, devices, or surgery to prevent pregnancy. There are many different types. Some are reversible, while others are permanent. Some types can also help prevent <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/sexuallytransmittedinfections.html">sexually transmitted infections</a> (STIs).</p>

<h3>What are the different types of birth control?</h3>
<p>There are several different types of birth control, and they work in different ways:</p>

<h3>What are some other forms of pregnancy prevention? </h3>
<p>There are some types of pregnancy prevention that do not involve medicines, devices, or surgery:</p>

<table border="0" class="info-table-1" width="90%">
<thead>
		<tr>
			<th colspan="2">Other forms of pregnancy prevention</th>
		</tr>
	</thead>
		<tbody><tr class="r1">
			<td>Fertility awareness-based methods</td>
			<td>They are also called natural rhythm methods. They involve tracking the woman's fertility cycle and avoiding sex or using barrier methods on the days when she is most likely to get pregnant. This method may have higher pregnancy rates than other types.</td>
		</tr>
		<tr class="r2">
			<td>Lactational amenorrhea method (LAM)</td>
			<td>A form of natural birth control for breastfeeding mothers. It relies on the new mother feeding her baby only breastmilk for up to six months and having no periods or spotting during that time.</td>
		</tr>
<tr class="r1">
			<td>Withdrawal</td>
			<td>During intercourse, the penis is pulled out of the vagina before ejaculation. The goal is to keep sperm from entering the vagina . But the sperm can leak out before the penis is pulled out, so this method has higher pregnancy rates than other types.</td>
		</tr>
	</tbody>
</table>

<h3>What is emergency contraception? </h3><p>Emergency contraception is not a regular method of birth control. But it can be used to prevent pregnancy after unprotected intercourse or if a condom breaks. There are two types:</p><ul>
<li>Copper IUD, which is a small, T-shaped device that a provider inserts within 120 hours of unprotected intercourse.</li> 
<li>Emergency contraceptive pills (ECPs), which are hormonal pills which the woman takes as soon as possible after unprotected intercourse.</li>
</ul><h3>What else should I know about birth control?</h3><p>When deciding which birth control type is right for you, there are many different factors to consider:</p><ul>
<li>Do you want to have children someday? How soon?</li>
<li>Do you have any health conditions?</li>
<li>How often do you have sex?</li>
<li>How many sex partners do you have?</li>
<li>Do you also need protection from HIV and other STIs?</li>
<li>How well does the birth control method work?</li>
<li>Are there any side effects?</li>
<li>Will you be able to use it correctly every time?</li>
<li>How much does it cost?</li>
</ul><p>Your provider can answer your questions about birth control to help you select the best type for you.</p>
