# Sleep Apnea

<p>Sleep apnea is a common disorder that causes your breathing to stop or get very shallow. Breathing pauses can last from a few seconds to minutes. They may occur 30 times or more an hour.</p>

<p>The most common type is obstructive sleep apnea. It causes your airway to collapse or become blocked during sleep. Normal breathing starts again with a snort or choking sound. People with sleep apnea often snore loudly. However, not everyone who <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/snoring.html">snores</a> has sleep apnea.</p>

<p>You are more at risk for sleep apnea if you are overweight, male, or have a family history or small airways.  Children with enlarged <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/tonsillitis.html">tonsils</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/adenoids.html">adenoids</a> may also get it.</p>

<p>Doctors diagnose sleep apnea based on medical and family histories, a physical exam, and sleep study results.</p>

<p>When your sleep is interrupted throughout the night, you can be drowsy during the day. People with sleep apnea are at higher risk for car crashes, work-related accidents, and other medical problems. If you have it, it is important to get treatment. Lifestyle changes, mouthpieces, surgery, and breathing devices can treat sleep apnea in many people.</p>
