# Developmental Disabilities

<h3>What are developmental disabilities?</h3>
<p>Developmental disabilities are conditions that are usually present at birth. They can affect a child's growth and development. These conditions can cause physical, learning, language, or behavior issues. They can include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/learningdisabilities.html">Learning disabilities</a>. These conditions affect the ability to learn.</li> 
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/autismspectrumdisorder.html">Autism spectrum disorder</a> (ASD). This is a disorder that affects behavior, communication, and social skills.</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/cerebralpalsy.html">Cerebral palsy</a> (CP). This is a condition that affects movement, coordination, and balance.</li>
<li>Physical disabilities. These could include <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/visionimpairmentandblindness.html">blindness</a> or <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/hearingdisordersanddeafness.html">deafness</a>.</li>
<li>Conditions that can affect both physical and mental abilities. This could include <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/downsyndrome.html">Down syndrome</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>These conditions can cause lifelong challenges in learning. Completing daily tasks may be difficult. Early treatment may help your child learn important skills and make the most of their strengths.</p>

<h3>What causes developmental disabilities?</h3>
<p>Developmental disabilities are often present at birth. They can occur due to various reasons. These can include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Genetic or chromosome abnormalities. These cause conditions such as Down syndrome, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fragilexsyndrome.html">Fragile-X-Syndrome</a>, and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/rettsyndrome.html">Rett syndrome</a>.</li>
<li>Use of substances while pregnant. For example, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/pregnancyandsubstanceuse.html">drinking alcohol when pregnant</a> can cause <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/fetalalcoholspectrumdisorders.html">fetal alcohol spectrum disorders</a>.</li>
<li>Certain <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/infectionsandpregnancy.html">infections during pregnancy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/prematurebabies.html">Preterm birth</a> or low <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/birthweight.html">birth weight</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3>How are developmental disabilities diagnosed?</h3>
<p>The way a developmental disability is diagnosed depends on the condition. Screening tests or a physical exam may be done to check for a medical problem. Other tests and questionnaires may help find out if your child has a developmental disability. These may include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Screening tests that are done during pregnancy or for newborns</li>
<li>Developmental and behavioral screening tests or questionnaires  to check if your child is developing on schedule</li>
<li>Observing your child's behavior and how they interact with other people</li>
<li>Testing your child's intellectual and school performance</li>
<li>Asking about your family history</li>
</ul>

<h3>What are the treatments for developmental disabilities?</h3>
<p>If your child has a developmental disability, these are usually lifelong conditions. Often, there is no cure, but treatment can help the symptoms. Treatments can include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Physical, speech, and occupational therapy</li> 
<li>Special teaching methods</li>
<li>Psychological counseling</li>
<li>To keep track of how your child grows and changes over time</li>
</ul>
