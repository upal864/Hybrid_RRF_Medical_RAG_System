# Dietary Fiber

<p>Fiber is a substance in plants. Dietary fiber is the kind you eat. It's a type of <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/carbohydrates.html">carbohydrate</a>. You may also see it listed on a food label as soluble fiber or insoluble fiber. Both types have important health benefits.</p>

<p>Good sources of dietary fiber include:</p><ul>
<li>Whole grains</li>
<li>Nuts and seeds</li>
<li>Fruit and vegetables</li>
</ul>

<p>Dietary fiber adds bulk to your diet and makes you feel full faster, helping you control your weight. It helps digestion and helps prevent <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/constipation.html">constipation</a>. Most Americans don't eat enough dietary fiber. But add it to your diet slowly. Increasing dietary fiber too quickly can lead to <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/gas.html">gas</a>, bloating, and cramps.</p>
