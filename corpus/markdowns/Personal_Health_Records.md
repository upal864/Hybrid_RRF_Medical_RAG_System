# Personal Health Records

<p>You've probably seen your chart at your doctor's office. In fact, you may have charts at several doctors' offices.  If you've been in the hospital, you have a chart there, too. These charts are your medical records. They may be on paper or electronic. To keep track of all this information, it's a good idea to keep your own personal health record.  </p>

<p>What kind of information would you put in a personal health record? You could start with:</p><ul>
<li>	Your name, birth date, blood type, and emergency contact information</li>
<li>	Date of last physical</li>
<li>	Dates and results of tests and screenings</li>
<li>	Major illnesses and surgeries, with dates</li>
<li>	A list of your medicines and supplements, the dosages, and how long you've taken them</li>
<li>	Any allergies</li>
<li>	Any chronic diseases</li>
<li>	Any history of illnesses in your family</li>
</ul>
