# Health Literacy

<h3>What is health literacy?</h3>
<p>Health literacy involves the information that people need to be able to make good decisions about health. There are two parts:</p><ul>
<li><strong>Personal health literacy</strong> is about how well a person can find and understand the health information and services that they need. It is also about using the information and services to make good health decisions.</li>
<li><strong>Organizational health literacy</strong> is about how well organizations help people find the health information and services that they need. It also includes helping them use that information to make good health decisions.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Which factors can affect health literacy?</h3>
<p>Many different factors can affect a person's health literacy, including their:</p><ul>
<li>Knowledge of medical words</li>
<li>Understanding of how the health care system works</li>
<li>Ability to communicate with health care providers</li>
<li>Ability to find health information, which may require computer skills</li>
<li>Reading, writing, and number skills</li>
<li>Personal factors, such as age, income, education, language abilities, and culture</li>
<li>Physical or mental limitations</li>
</ul>

<p>Many of the same people who are at risk for limited health literacy also have <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/healthdisparities.html">health disparities</a>. Health disparities are health differences between different groups of people. These groups may be based on age, race, sex, or other factors.</p>

<h3>Why is health literacy important?</h3>
<p>Health literacy is important because it can affect your ability to:</p><ul>
<li>Make good decisions about your health.</li>
<li>Get the medical care you need. This includes preventative care, which is care that focuses on preventing disease and keeping you healthy.</li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/medicationerrors.html">Take your medicines correctly</a>.</li>
<li>Manage a disease, especially a <a href="copingwithchronicillness.html" tid="4208">chronic (long-term) disease</a>.</li>
<li>Lead a healthy lifestyle.</li>
</ul>

<p>One thing that you can do is to make sure that you <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/talkingwithyourdoctor.html">communicate well with your health care providers</a>. If you don't understand something a provider tells you, ask them to explain it to you so that you understand. You can also ask the provider to write down their instructions.</p>
